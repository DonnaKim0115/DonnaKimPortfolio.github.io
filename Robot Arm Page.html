Robot Arm

What?
A group project made for the University of Waterloo's first-year "Tron Day" event.
A robot arm with 2 degrees of freedom, made using a Tetrix Robotics kits. The robot was tasked to lift 3D printed bones and place them in corresponding slots located on an elevated wall.

How Does it Work?
The robot arm was centered on a moving base fixed with 2 continuous motors that were remote controlled, giving the robot the first degree of freedom. The arm was lowered and raised using a gear system connected to a servo motor, giving the second degree of freedom. The robot hand itself was controlled by another servo motor to open and close the hand. All motors were connected to the Tetrix Kit control board, which was wirelessly paired to a controller.
